<div class="notice--primary">
  <h3>Visiting Research — Tohoku University (TESP 2025)</h3>
  <p>I joined the <strong>Space Robotics Lab</strong> at Tohoku University (Sendai, Japan) for a visiting research placement in June–July 2025 under the supervision of <a href="https://astro.mech.tohoku.ac.jp/e/">Prof. Kazuya Yoshida</a>. My research focused on autonomous lunar navigation and robot interaction: designing Dijkstra-based path planners, integrating visual object detection for robust in-field perception, and developing a custom robot platform including gripper control and onboard planning.</p>

  <p style="margin-top:.5rem">During the program I led the <strong>MoonBot Navigation</strong> project (linked below); the project won the <strong>TESP 2025 Competition</strong> and I was selected as the top student of the exchange.</p>

## MoonBot Navigation

- **Repository:** <a href="https://github.com/alessioborgi/MoonBot-Navigation" target="_blank" rel="noopener">MoonBot Navigation (GitHub)</a>
- **Summary:** Autonomous navigation and object interaction for a lunar rover prototype. Key components included a Dijkstra-based planner for waypoint navigation, camera-based object detection and tracking for obstacle avoidance and task triggering, and custom gripper actuation.
- **Result:** Winner of the TESP 2025 Competition (Tohoku University).
